MERIDIAN knows this group’s average view. It does not know how many individual port workers agree, disagree or remain unsure.

An average can hide what is really happening. A score of 5 out of 10 could mean everyone is unsure — or that half strongly agree and half strongly disagree. MERIDIAN does not know which, so it does not draw one.

Groups are not people. A population group is a broad-scale instrument: it carries population weight, exposure coverage and one average view. It cannot tell you about any individual, and MERIDIAN never claims that everyone in it believes the same thing. Named individuals are modelled separately, on the roster. E
